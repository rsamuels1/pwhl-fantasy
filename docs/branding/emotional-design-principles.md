# Emotional Design Principles — PWHL GM

## North Star

> PWHL GM should feel like checking the standings on an arena concourse between periods, not configuring accounting software.

---

## Should feel

- **Competitive** — Every number on screen means something. Scores have context. Gaps matter.
- **Welcoming** — New to hockey? New to fantasy? The product never punishes curiosity.
- **Premium** — Clean, intentional, earned. Not flashy — confident.
- **Seasonal** — The interface breathes with the calendar. Off-season ≠ in-season ≠ playoffs.
- **Social** — The fun lives in the matchup, the trash talk, the comeback. Surface those moments.

## Must never feel

- Enterprise SaaS
- Hockey analytics software
- Generic admin dashboard
- Mobile banking app

---

## The Gold Rule (prestige moments)

Use `--gold` exactly four places: the champion card, the clinch banner, the high-score badge, and the hot-streak chip. Nowhere else. Gold means something because it's rare.

Never put gold on buttons, nav items, or repeated UI chrome.

---

## Empty states

Empty states should feel like an open ice rink before practice — anticipation, not absence. Use warm copy that points forward ("Your roster is empty — draft players to fill it") rather than cold error language ("No data found").

---

## Scoring education

Every jargon term (FP, VP, PPP, UTIL) earns its presence by being explained inline on first use. Tooltips are the preferred surface. Remove the term before adding a tooltip — only keep both if they're both necessary.

---

## Signature moments

These are the arena-cheering moments the product should celebrate:

1. **Draft pick made** — the pick cell populates with energy
2. **Playoff clinch** — gold banner, dismissible, one-time
3. **Weekly high score** — gold badge in the highlights
4. **Championship** — prestige gradient on the champion card, permanent

Tone for positive moments: direct, celebratory, not sycophantic.
Tone for negative moments: honest, with a recovery path. Never shame.
