I went through the handoff package, the README, token docs, inventory, and the preview screens. As a user, this redesign conveys a much stronger identity than just “a fantasy sports tool.”

What this redesign communicates to me as a user

👍 It feels like a game first, spreadsheet second

The current direction tells me:

* This is a fantasy league experience people check every day.
* Winning, competing, and progressing are supposed to feel rewarding.
* There is a commissioner/social layer, not just roster management.
* The app wants to create anticipation (“Live now”, playoff race, weekly leaders).

Compared to the old “Bloomberg terminal” description, I think you’re moving in the right direction.

The matchup screen especially feels much more like:

“I’m following my team”

instead of

“I’m auditing statistics.”

⸻

👍 The color palette is much friendlier

The sky blue accent + graphite surfaces work well.

The gold usage is smart because it introduces emotional hierarchy.

My reaction:

* Green = good
* Red = bad
* Blue = actionable
* Gold = special

That maps naturally.

⸻

👍 The handoff itself is excellent technically

As an engineer receiving this, I know exactly what to do.

You provide:

* design philosophy
* implementation instructions
* token mappings
* inventory
* replacement strategy
* examples
* edge cases
* contrast notes

That’s honestly better than many Figma handoffs.

⸻

What I think users still perceive

Looking at the screenshots alone, I still think:

“This is a serious fantasy hockey management app.”

I don’t yet think:

“This is exciting.”

or

“This is a place where things happen.”

The redesign improves usability and warmth, but not necessarily energy.

⸻

Suggestions to make it more engaging

1. Introduce one signature accent color

Right now everything is very tasteful.

Possibly too tasteful.

Apps people remember usually have one memorable color moment.

Examples:

* Discord → blurple
* Sleeper → neon lime
* ESPN Fantasy → bright red
* DraftKings → green
* Spotify → green

You already have gold.

I’d lean into it harder.

Examples:

* League champion card glow
* First place badge
* Hot streak indicator
* “League leader this week”

Not everywhere.

Just enough so users subconsciously associate:

Gold = prestige

⸻

2. Add more personality to empty states

The screenshots show polished dashboards.

But empty states often define whether an app feels alive.

Examples:

No trade offers

📭 Quiet week.

Nobody’s shopping players right now.

⸻

No lineup changes

✅ You’re all set.

Sit back and let your stars cook.

⸻

Draft queue empty

📝 Build your wishlist before draft night.

Tiny copy moments make a surprisingly large difference.

⸻

3. The matchup page should feel more dramatic

This screen is probably your highest traffic page.

Current feeling:

Professional

Desired feeling:

Competitive

Ideas:

Small trend arrows

▲ +4.3 projected
▼ -1.2 projected

Momentum chip

🔥 3 players still skating
❄️ Opponent has finished

Projected upset callout

⚡ 18% chance to steal the win

⸻

4. Consider adding team identity colors

Everything currently uses system colors.

I wonder if managers should have tiny bits of customization.

Examples:

* avatar ring color
* banner tint
* subtle gradient behind team name

Not enough to become noisy.

Just enough for users to recognize opponents instantly.

⸻

5. The wizard still feels a little utilitarian

The rules page is clean, but emotionally it feels like settings.

Maybe add a lightweight summary panel:

⸻

Your league at a glance

🏒 8 teams

📅 20 weeks

🎯 Head-to-head points

💰 Free agency every Sunday

“This should take about 2 minutes.”

⸻

It reframes configuration as creating an experience.

Feedback on the handoff package itself

I’d add a short section near the top:

Design principles

PWHL GM should feel:

* Competitive
* Welcoming
* Premium
* Seasonal
* Social

Avoid feeling:

* Enterprise SaaS
* Hockey analytics software
* Generic admin dashboard
* Bloomberg terminal
* Mobile banking app

That gives designers and engineers a north star when making future decisions.

Overall impression

If I stumbled onto these screens without context, I’d assume this app is:

A polished fantasy hockey platform somewhere between Sleeper and ESPN Fantasy, aimed at dedicated fans who enjoy managing leagues over an entire season.

The redesign succeeds at making it look more premium and approachable. The next step isn’t making it prettier—it’s making it feel more alive, competitive, and emotionally rewarding when users win, climb standings, or interact with other managers.
