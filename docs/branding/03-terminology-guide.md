# PWHL GM Terminology Guide

**Quick reference for copy/UX text throughout the app.**

## At a Glance

| Context | Before | After | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|-------|
| Product name | PWHL Fantasy | PWHL GM | Use everywhere |
| User description | Fantasy player | General Manager / GM | Reframe users as executives |
| Team reference | Fantasy team | Franchise | More executive feel |
| Team ownership | Own a team | Run a franchise | Management language |
| Points | Fantasy points | [Context-specific]* | Remove "fantasy" modifier |
| Interface | Fantasy app | Management platform | Marketing/landing pages |
| Commissioner tools | Admin panel / Settings | Front Office | Executive/leadership framing |
| Leaderboard rank | Standings | Standings (unchanged) | Brief-approved term |
| Draft interface | Draft / Draft room | Draft Room | Brief-approved term |
| Roster | Roster (unchanged) | Roster (unchanged) | Brief-approved term |
| Matchup | Matchup (unchanged) | Matchup (unchanged) | Brief-approved term |
| League | League (unchanged) | League (unchanged) | Brief-approved term |

*Points: Use "pts", "Championship points", or "VP" (Victory Points) depending on context. Avoid "fantasy points" in UI.

### Tone Examples

#### ❌ Avoid (Fantasy Sports Tone)
- "Join our fantasy league and score big!"
- "Pick your fantasy stars and dominate."
- "Fantasy points = bragging rights."
- "The top fantasy scorer wins the jackpot."

#### ✅ Prefer (Management Tone)
- "Build a championship franchise and compete for the title."
- "Scout players, set your strategy, and lead your team to victory."
- "Championship points drive your standings rank."
- "The best General Manager wins the championship."

## Dos and Don'ts

### ✅ DO

- Use "General Manager" and "GM" throughout
- Reference "franchises" when discussing user teams
- Frame features as "executive tools" or "management decisions"
- Use "Draft Room" for the live draft interface
- Emphasize championships, standings, and playoffs
- Position waiver wire as "player management" not "luck-based trades"

### ❌ DON'T

- Use "fantasy" as a modifier (except in historical context)
- Describe users as "players" or "fantasy players" (they are GMs)
- Use gambling/betting language ("odds", "payout", "win big")
- Call it "fantasy app" or "fantasy sports" in marketing
- Overuse "team" — prefer "franchise"
- Use casino-associated colors (neon green, bright red, orange)

## Writing Examples by Feature

### Draft Copy

❌ **Wrong:** "Join our fantasy draft and pick the best players!"  
✅ **Right:** "Scout top talent in the Draft Room and build your championship roster."

### Scoring/Stats

❌ **Wrong:** "Earn fantasy points for goals and assists."  
✅ **Right:** "Earn Championship points for goals and assists."

### Standings

❌ **Wrong:** "Check your fantasy ranking."  
✅ **Right:** "Check your standings and playoff race position."

### Lineup

❌ **Wrong:** "Set your fantasy lineup for this week."  
✅ **Right:** "Set your lineup and manage your roster for this week."

### Admin/Commissioner

❌ **Wrong:** "Manage the fantasy league settings."  
✅ **Right:** "Run your league from the Front Office."

## Enforcement

- [ ] All "fantasy" references removed from public-facing copy
- [ ] Logo updated to shield + GM

- [ ] Dashboard headers use "Franchises" language (at minimum)
- [ ] Help text and onboarding use management framing

### Post-Launch Guidelines

For any new features or copy added:

1. **Use the terms table above** as reference
2. **Read aloud** to ensure tone sounds executive, not gamer-y
3. **Avoid "fantasy"** — use specific descriptors (Championship, VP, pts)
4. **Emphasize strategy** — "decide", "build", "lead" over "claim", "grab", "score"
5. **Reference the brief** if uncertain
