PWHL GM Product Recommendations

Post-Launch Product Evolution Roadmap

Version: 1.0

⸻

Executive Summary

PWHL GM has reached feature maturity as a fantasy platform.

The next phase of development should focus less on adding capabilities and more on surfacing existing systems through storytelling, recognition, and league identity.

The goal is to evolve PWHL GM from:

“Fantasy hockey software”

into

“A living hockey league that remembers things, celebrates accomplishments, and tells stories about itself.”

⸻

Product North Star

PWHL GM should feel like walking through an arena concourse between periods.

Managers should encounter:

📰 Headlines

🏆 Trophies

🔥 Streaks

📈 Playoff races

🥇 Records

⚔️ Rivalries

⭐ Awards

📚 League history

It should never feel like configuring accounting software.

⸻

Highlights

The challenge is not lack of features.

The challenge is discoverability.

Many compelling experiences are distributed across isolated pages.

The next phase should consolidate these systems into league narratives.

Final Recommendation

PWHL GM has enough functionality.

Future work should prioritize emotional engagement.

Every feature should answer one question:

“Will this give managers another reason to open the app today?”

If the answer is yes,

it belongs on the roadmap.

One additional thought after sleeping on the repo a bit: I think The Morning Skate may actually deserve to be the app’s first truly branded subsystem, with its own icon, archive page, and navigation entry. If users start saying “I checked the Morning Skate” instead of “I opened PWHL GM,” you’ve created something people identify with rather than simply use.
