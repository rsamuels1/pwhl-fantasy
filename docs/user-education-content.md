# User Education Content

Version: 1.0

Purpose: Explain PWHL Fantasy concepts to managers, especially users who have never played fantasy sports before.

---

# Welcome to PWHL Fantasy

PWHL Fantasy lets you draft professional women's hockey players and compete against friends throughout the season.

Each week your players earn fantasy points based on real-world performance.

Your goal:

Score more points than your opponent and climb the standings.

---

# How a Fantasy League Works

1. Join a league
2. Draft players
3. Set your lineup each week
4. Compete in matchups
5. Earn VP
6. Qualify for playoffs
7. Win a championship

---

# Rosters

Default roster:

3 Forwards

2 Defense

1 Utility

1 Goalie

6 Bench

---

# Draft Basics

Every manager takes turns selecting players.

Once drafted:

Only your team owns those players.

Draft order follows a snake format.

Example:

Round 1:

1 → 8

Round 2:

8 → 1

---

# Weekly Lineups

Each week you choose:

- 3 Forwards
- 2 Defense
- 1 Utility
- 1 Goalie

Bench players do not score.

---

# Weekly Lineup Lock

Lineups lock weekly.

You may still make changes during the matchup week if:

The replacement player has not yet played.

You cannot swap in players after their game has started.

---

# How Scoring Works

Players earn fantasy points based on real PWHL statistics.

Examples include:

- Goals
- Assists
- Saves
- Wins

Fantasy points accumulate throughout the matchup week.

---

# What Is VP?

VP stands for Victory Points.

Unlike traditional fantasy leagues, standings are based on more than just wins and losses.

---

# Why VP Exists

Traditional fantasy leagues can be unfair.

Example:

You score the second-highest point total all week.

Unfortunately:

You happen to face the highest-scoring team.

You lose despite having a great week.

VP reduces that problem.

---

# How VP Works

Each week managers earn VP from:

## Matchup Results

Winning your matchup earns VP.

---

## Weekly Performance

Additional VP is awarded based on your rank among all teams that week.

This rewards strong performance even when schedule luck is against you.

---

# VP Example

Eight-team league:

You win your matchup.

You also finish second in weekly scoring.

Result:

Matchup VP + Weekly Ranking VP

Your standings improve more than a simple win/loss system.

---

# Standings

Standings are determined by:

1. Total VP
2. Tiebreakers
3. Points scored

---

# Playoffs

Default format:

4 teams qualify.

Semi-finals:

1 vs 4

2 vs 3

Winners advance.

Champion wins the league.

---

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Do I Need Hockey Knowledge?

No.

Many successful fantasy managers learn during the season.

---

## Can I Change My Lineup Every Day?

No.

The league uses weekly lineup management.

---

## Why Did My Player Lock?

Their game already started.

Locked players cannot be swapped retroactively.

---

## Why Did I Gain VP Even Though I Lost?

Weekly ranking VP rewards strong overall performance.

---

## What Happens If I Miss The Draft?

The system automatically selects players for you.

---

# Beginner Tips

1. Set your lineup every week.
2. Draft players who play regularly.
3. Don't panic after one bad week.
4. Pay attention to standings.
5. Have fun competing with friends.

Fantasy hockey is a season-long game.
