PWHL Fantasy UX Audit (Post-Matchup & Home Redesign)

Executive Summary

The product has crossed an important threshold.

Previous audits identified that the application felt more like fantasy league management software than a fantasy sports product.

The updated screenshots show substantial improvement:

* Fantasy Home exists
* Matchups are more visible
* Team management feels more natural
* Mobile navigation is significantly improved
* Commissioner functionality is less dominant
* The application feels closer to ESPN Fantasy and Sleeper

The next phase is no longer about fixing architecture.

The next phase is about increasing engagement, urgency, and daily return behavior.

⸻

Overall Product Assessment

Current State

The application now feels like:

Fantasy Hockey Platform

rather than:

Fantasy League Administration Software

This is a major improvement.

⸻

Current Strengths

Fantasy Home

The dashboard is now much closer to a true fantasy home.

Positive observations:

* Action Needed section exists
* Team shortcuts exist
* Matchup entry points exist
* Navigation feels more focused

Users now have a clearer understanding of:

“What should I do next?”

⸻

Team Management

Roster and lineup pages have improved substantially.

Strengths:

* Card-based presentation
* Better visual hierarchy
* Less spreadsheet-like
* Easier mobile consumption

The team experience is no longer the primary concern.

⸻

Mobile Experience

Bottom navigation was an excellent addition.

This immediately makes the application feel more like a fantasy app and less like a traditional website.

This should remain the long-term mobile navigation model.

⸻

Highest Priority Remaining Opportunity

Fantasy Home 2.0

The current home page is good.

The next goal is making it indispensable.

Current home page answers:

* What teams do I have?
* What actions exist?

The next version should answer:

* What happened?
* What matters today?
* What should I do right now?

⸻

Recommended Home Page Structure

Section 1: Action Needed

Always first.

Examples:

* Lineup lock approaching
* Empty lineup slot
* Draft starts soon
* Waivers processing tonight
* Bench player active tonight

These should be treated as the highest-priority information in the app.

⸻

Section 2: Active Matchups

For every league:

Show:

* Current score
* Projected score
* Win probability
* Remaining players
* Quick access to matchup

This should become the dominant content area.

⸻

Section 3: Tonight’s Games

Example:

Tonight

Montreal vs Minnesota

You have:

* Taylor Heise
* Sarah Fillier

2 active players tonight

This creates a reason to watch games.

⸻

Section 4: Fantasy Headlines

Examples:

* Taylor Heise scored 18.4 fantasy points
* Sarah Fillier on a 4-game point streak
* Hilary Knight trending upward

Focus on fantasy relevance.

Not general hockey news.

⸻

Section 5: League Activity

League activity should remain visible but should not dominate the home page.

Final Assessment

The application has successfully transitioned from:

“League management platform”

to

“Fantasy sports platform”

The next stage is transitioning from:

“Fantasy sports platform”

to

“Fantasy sports habit”

The primary product goal should now be:

Create reasons for users to open the app multiple times per day.

Every future feature should be evaluated against this question:

“Does this help users feel more connected to their matchup, their players, or their league today?”

If not, it should be deprioritized.
