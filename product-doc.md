# Executive Summary

PWHL Fantasy is a fantasy hockey platform built specifically for the Professional Women's Hockey League (PWHL).

Users create leagues with friends, draft real PWHL players, set lineups throughout the season, and compete in head-to-head weekly matchups based on real-world player performance.

The platform is designed around three core experiences:

1. **Drafting a team**
2. **Managing a roster during the season**
3. **Competing against other managers in weekly matchups**

A unique feature of the platform is **Historical Replay Mode**, which allows users to run a fantasy season using completed historical PWHL seasons and advance through the season at their own pace.

# How the App Works

## Step 1: Create a League

A commissioner creates a fantasy league and configures:

- League name
- Number of teams
- Roster settings
- Scoring rules
- Draft settings
- Playoff structure

The commissioner can then invite friends to join.

---

## Step 2: Join a League

Each manager joins the league and receives ownership of a fantasy team.

Before the season begins, every manager enters the draft with an empty roster.

---

## Step 3: Draft Players

The draft is the foundation of the fantasy season.

Managers take turns selecting real PWHL players.

The platform supports:

- Snake drafts
- Live draft clock
- Draft queue management
- Auto-draft support
- Real-time updates for all participants

Managers can:

- Search players
- Filter by position
- View prior-season statistics
- Build personal draft queues
- Track roster needs during the draft

---

## Step 4: Build Your Roster

After the draft, managers maintain rosters that typically include:

- Forwards
- Defense
- Goalies
- Utility slots
- Bench players

Throughout the season, managers decide which players are active and which remain on the bench.

---

## Step 5: Set Your Lineup

Before games begin, managers set their active lineup.

Only active players contribute fantasy points.

Lineup management includes:

- Starting players
- Benching players
- Position validation
- Player lock protection once games begin

This mirrors how real fantasy sports platforms operate.

---

## Step 6: Weekly Matchups

Managers compete head-to-head.

Each scoring period compares one fantasy team against another.

The team that accumulates more fantasy points wins the matchup.

The standings update automatically throughout the season.

---

## Step 7: Reach the Playoffs

At the end of the regular season:

- Top teams qualify
- Brackets are generated automatically
- Matchups continue through playoff rounds
- A league champion is crowned

Commissioners can customize:

- Number of playoff teams
- Bye structure
- Round lengths
- Tiebreak rules

---

# Fantasy Scoring

Fantasy points are generated from real PWHL player statistics.

Examples include:

### Skaters

- Goals
- Assists
- Power-play points
- Shots on goal
- Hits
- Blocked shots

### Goalies

- Wins
- Saves
- Goals against
- Save percentage
- Shutouts

The platform calculates fantasy scores directly from game statistics.

This means scoring can be adjusted without losing historical results.

---

# Matchup Experience

The platform is designed around a "matchup-first" philosophy.

Managers see:

- Current score
- Opponent score
- Top performers
- Projected outcomes
- Win probability
- Key lineup decisions
- Storylines and momentum

The objective is to make every matchup feel meaningful and easy to follow.

# Historical Replay Mode

## What Is It?

Historical Replay Mode allows managers to run fantasy leagues using a completed PWHL season.

Instead of waiting for future games to happen, users replay a past season using actual historical results.

The current implementation is built around the 2025–26 PWHL season.

---

## Why It Matters

Replay Mode solves several challenges:

### New User Onboarding

Users can immediately experience a full fantasy season.

No need to wait for the next real-world season.

### Year-Round Engagement

The platform remains playable during the offseason.

### Testing and League Experiments

Commissioners can test:

- New scoring systems
- Different roster structures
- Alternative playoff formats

### Historical "What If?" Scenarios

Managers can ask questions like:

- What if I drafted Sarah Fillier first?
- Could I build a better roster than last season?
- Which players were fantasy MVPs?

---

## How Replay Works

### Create a Replay League

The commissioner selects:

**Replay League (2025–26 Season)**

The platform loads:

- Historical teams
- Historical players
- Historical schedules
- Historical game results

---

### Conduct the Draft

Managers draft players normally.

The draft experience is identical to a live season.

A special auto-draft option is also available.

Auto-draft can instantly populate rosters using historical fantasy rankings.

---

### Advance Through Time

Instead of waiting for games to occur, the commissioner advances the replay timeline.

Each advance:

- Moves the league forward
- Processes completed games
- Calculates fantasy scores
- Updates standings
- Advances scoring periods

The league effectively "replays" history.

---

### Experience the Season

Managers still:

- Set lineups
- Monitor matchups
- Track standings
- Make roster decisions

The difference is that the season progresses according to historical results.

---

## Benefits of Replay Mode

### Immediate Fun

No waiting for the next season.

### Education

Learn fantasy strategy using known outcomes.

### Commissioner Sandbox

Test league configurations safely.

### Product Demonstrations

Ideal for showcasing the platform to new users and investors.
