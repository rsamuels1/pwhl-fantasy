Brand Strategy & Identity Guide

Last Updated: June 2026

⸻

Executive Summary

PWHL General Manager (“PWHL GM”) is a fantasy sports platform designed specifically for fans of the Professional Women’s Hockey League.

Unlike traditional fantasy products that focus primarily on drafting players and accumulating points, PWHL GM positions users as hockey executives responsible for building, managing, and competing with a professional-caliber roster.

The brand should feel:

* Professional
* Strategic
* Competitive
* Modern
* League-aligned

The long-term goal is to create a product and identity that could be presented as an official fantasy experience for the PWHL.


# Brand Positioning

Core Idea

Fans are not simply fantasy players.

They are General Managers.

The product allows users to:

* Draft players
* Build rosters
* Set lineups
* Make strategic decisions
* Compete for championships

Every feature should reinforce the feeling of running a hockey organization.

# Brand Personality

- Strategic

The platform is about decision making.

Not luck.

Not gambling.

Not arcade-style gaming.

- Professional

The experience should resemble a hockey front office.

Users should feel like executives rather than gamers.

- Modern

Design language should be clean and contemporary.

Avoid outdated sports design trends.

- Competitive

Championships, standings, playoffs, and rivalry should be emphasized.

- Credible

The platform should look like something that belongs alongside official PWHL properties.

# Target Audience

- Primary

PWHL Fans

Users who follow the league and want deeper engagement.

- Secondary

Fantasy Hockey Players

Existing fantasy players looking for a PWHL-focused experience.

- Future

League Partners

Sponsors

Media Organizations

The branding should remain professional enough for commercial partnerships.

# Visual Identity

Design Philosophy

The visual identity should feel closer to:

* Professional sports leagues
* Front-office operations
* Championship culture

and less like:

* Daily fantasy sports
* Sports betting
* Esports organizations

### Logo System

Primary Concept: Modern Shield

A shield communicates:

* Competition
* Authority
* Championships
* Professional sports

The shield should become the core visual asset for the brand.

#### Logo Direction A

GM Monogram Shield

Structure:

     _______
    /       \
   |   GM    |
   |         |
    \_______/

Characteristics:

* Large stylized GM letters
* Minimal visual noise
* Strong app icon
* Strong favicon
* Easy recognition

Ice-Cut Shield

Structure:

Modern shield featuring subtle geometric cuts inspired by:

* Ice markings
* Rink geometry
* Skate paths

* Premium appearance
* Hockey-specific without being obvious
* Future-proof design

Priority: VERY HIGH

Preferred long-term logo direction.

⸻

#### Logo Elements To Avoid

Avoid:

* Hockey sticks
* Hockey pucks
* Player silhouettes
* Cartoon mascots
* Flames
* Wings
* Generic fantasy trophies

These create a fantasy-sports appearance rather than a professional sports-management appearance.

⸻

Color System

Primary Colors

Retain existing palette where possible.

Recommended:

Deep Purple

Primary brand color.

Represents connection to the broader PWHL ecosystem.

⸻

Dark Navy

Primary application background.

Professional and modern.

⸻

White

Primary text and contrast color.

⸻

Accent Colors

Use sparingly.

Examples:

* Silver
* Light gray
* Ice blue

Should support the primary palette rather than compete with it.

⸻

Colors To Avoid

Avoid:

* Neon green
* Bright orange
* Bright red
* Casino-inspired color schemes

These shift the brand toward gambling and gaming.

# Long-Term Brand Vision

PWHL GM should become the definitive management and fantasy experience for women’s professional hockey.

The brand should be capable of supporting:

* Public leagues
* Private leagues
* Historical seasons
* Dynasty leagues
* Hall of Fame systems
* Commissioner tools
* Mobile applications
* Official PWHL partnerships

Every branding decision should reinforce the core promise:

Think Like a GM.

That statement should remain true regardless of future features, league partnerships, or product expansions.
